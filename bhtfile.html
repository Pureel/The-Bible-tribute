The Bible; the Word of God

Here's a timeline outlining the key events in the formation of the Bible:

  • 1400-400BC- The books of the Old Testament were written by prophets,leaders and scribes mostly in Hebrew with some parts in Aramaic. These include Genesis, Psalms, Isiah, Daniel etc.
  • 45-100AD-The Books of the New Testament were written by eyewitnesses of Jesus and close followers in Greek. They include the Gospels, Acts etc.
  • 300s AD-Church leaders confirmed the list of inspired books and the current 27-book New Testament(The Canon Formation)
  • ~400AD-The Latin Vulgate becomes the main Bible in Europe. Copies were handwritten in Latin, mostly for priests.
  • 1455AD-The Gutenberg Bible; The first Bible to be printed with a press, was published in Latin
  • 1500s AD-Reformers like Martin Luther (German) and William Tyndale translated the Bible into common languages
  • 1611-The King James Version was published and was very influential
  • 1900s-2000s-Many modern translations like NIV,ESV etc. emerged, aiming for accessiblity and readability
  • Today-The Bible has been translated into over 3,500 languages and is available in print, audio, apps, and online. It is still the most widely read and distributed book in the world. Yay :)
